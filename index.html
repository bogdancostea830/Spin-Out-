An archetypal glossary of bangers you may not know.
Spin Out! is a resource for music fans to expand their tastes and their libraries. From the super popular stuff, borrowed from the Rolling Stone’s top 500 albums of all time, the archive branches out to lesser known artists and albums, structured in a virtual cork board. If you follow the thread, you might wind up in a completely new genre from where you started (and like it!)

Start by finding which of these nine archetypes speaks to you.

The Classic Collector

You think Zeppelin, Queen, and The Beatles were the greatest bands of all time (and I’m not here to tell you that’s wrong.) You love albums like Abbey Road, The Wall, and The Doors. Albums and artists you enjoy have an unmistakable twang and groove characteristic of analog recording and production techniques. Gotta love that grain!

Sounds about right >>>

The Crowd Surfer

Between the aviators and the bangs down to your chin, no one can tell where you’re looking. There are more patches on your denim vest than there are stars in the sky. You love to get active and, frankly, violent at concerts. Distorted guitars and badass riffs are mandatory for any piece of music that should attempt to cross your ears.

Sounds about right >>>

The Math Teacher

Big Rush fan. Your black jeans are practically glued to your legs. You love Dream Theater and Pink Floyd; you spin Dark Side of the Moon at least once a week. You have a strong appreciation for odd time signatures, complex compositions, and techniques and instruments that may not be common in the regular rock palette of sounds.

Sounds about right >>>

The Folk Hero

You have an old guitar, a cheap harmonica, and something to say; and your playlist shows it. You love Bob Dylan, Simon & Garfunkel, and you’ll even dabble in country when it’s Dolly Parton singing. You value the message over the music, preferring minimalist and acoustic arrangements with quality lyrics to overcomplicated compositions.

Sounds about right >>>

The Indie Head

You’ve had Getting Killed playing on repeat since it came out. You’re familiar with every artist currently putting out music; finding you something new is going to be challenging. Although, from Radiohead to mk.gee, you’re open to certain stranger sounds than the average joe, and that makes my job a little easier.

Sounds about right >>>

The Grunge Guy

Your soul belongs in 90’s Seattle. You’re spinning Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Alice In Chains, etc. You own like five different flannels and not a single pair of jeans without holes in them. You love loud, sloppy, distorted guitars and mumbled lyrics about suicide (which is, like, yeah fair enough).

Sounds about right >>>
