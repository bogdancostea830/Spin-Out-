Spin Out!

The Indie Head

You’ve had Getting Killed playing on repeat since it came out. You’re familiar with every artist currently putting out music; finding you something new is going to be challenging. Although, from Radiohead to mk.gee, you’re open to certain stranger sounds than the average joe, and that makes my job a little easier.

Fleetwood Mac - Rumours (1977)

Well deserving of its #7 spot on the Rolling Stone list, there is not one of Rumours’ 11 tracks that isn’t amazing. The stylistic range of the record from “Dreams” to “Don’t Stop” to “The Chain” is exemplary, all the while it still manages to feel uniform and complete.

Red Hot Chilli Peppers - Californication (1999)

Every bassist studies Flea, every guitarist studies Frusciante, and every drummer studies Smith. At #286 on the Rolling Stone list, Californication represents the best of one of the most important bands to have come out of the 90’s, with iconic riffs, masterful craftsmanship, and a lyrical reference to the state of California in nearly every song. This one is a must-hear.

Simon & Garfunkel - Bridge Over Troubled Water (1970)

One of the most iconic works from THE most iconic folk duo of all time, Bridge Over Troubled Water sits at #172 on the Rolling Stone list. It earns this position through banger after banger, including a title track so awesome that Elvis Presley covered it. Fun fact for those who didn’t know, Presley was one of the artists that inspired Simon & Garfunkel since their high school days. That’s pretty neat.

Radiohead - OK Computer (1997)

There are four Radiohead albums on the Rolling Stone list, and this isn’t even the highest, but it’s my favorite. At #42, OK Computer is famous for tracks like “Karma Police,” “Exit Music (For A Film),” and “No Surprises,” but the B-sides are equally awesome, like for instance “Electioneering.” Picking between this and In Rainbows was really hard. You should probably just listen to both. And The Bends. And Kid A.

Beck - Odelay (1996)

Beck’s discography is really fascinating, looking at the stylistic diversity from his earliest albums to his recent stuff, going from experimental (and borderline unlistenable) to very conventional (but still very good) pop music. Odelay lands on the earlier side, during his explorations into hip hop following Mellow Gold and the success of “Loser,” and through thoughtful compositions and tasteful deviancies, it earns its place on the Rolling Stone list at #424.

Elliott Smith - Either/Or (1997)

An absolutely devastating record from one of the most widely-known indie artists of all time. Either/Or earns its #216 spot on the Rolling Stone list through its emotion and craftsmanship, with vocals that, while produced, still manage to feel intimate and hit right where it hurts. For best results, enjoy at night, in stereo, in a dimly lit room, whilst gazing longingly out the window.

Björk - Homogenic (1997)

Björk manages to create so much atmosphere in every track and produce a sound that is so beautiful while also challenging us as listeners for the mere fact that she doesn’t really care for the rules and formulas of mainstream music. At #202 on the Rolling Stone list, Homogenic represents the best of Björk’s creative endeavours. I personally like Post better, but that’s mostly for the album cover.

Portishead - Dummy (1994)

“Sour Times,” “Roads,” and “Glory Box” are only a few of the certified bangers on this trip hop masterpiece, which occupies the #131 spot on the Rolling Stone list. The instrumentation is layered and atmospheric, the vocals are gorgeous with just the right amount of reverb, and together they create an intoxicatingly relaxing vibe. I have recommendations to enhance your experience with it, but this is a school assignment.

My Bloody Valentine - Loveless (1991)

A genre-defining band’s best known work, Loveless is required reading for anyone getting into shoegaze or dreampop. The entire album is amazingly well made, with layers and layers building up a nebulous, atmospheric sound that is somehow still so intentional. You haven’t lived until you’ve listened to “When You Sleep” out of insomnia and desperation at 4:00 in the morning.

Peach Pit - Magpie (2024)

Peach Pit is one of the more successful indie bands to have come from Vancouver in recent times, and this is owed to their polished, extremely well crafted sound. Magpie especially shows clear influence from The Beatles’ earlier sound, but still with a flair that is unique to them. The riff at the start of “St. Mark’s Funny Feeling” is straight out of Abbey Road.

Summer Salt - Happy Camper (2018)

Self-described as “trop-pop”, Summer Salt employs SoCal feelgoodery with clean, classic-sounding guitars for a sound that goes very well with a glass of lemonade and a chaise longue on a sunny summer day. Their subsequent albums drift into a darker vibe, which is certainly also worth listening to, but Happy Camper’s easy listening is what makes it my personal favorite.

Hot Freaks - Hot Freaks (2013)

Modern indie doo-wop. Hot Freaks have an upbeat, feelgood sound that is sure to resonate with any fans of the earlier Beatles, Buddy Holly, The Kinks, and other such poppier rock artists. They combine a 50’s attitude with 21st century indie rock grooves and sounds to produce music that will cure any acute negative feeling.

awakebutstillinbed - what people call low self-esteem is really just seeing yourself the way that other people see you (2018)

An impressive emo solo project that feels sad and angry and intimate all at once. Shannon Taylor’s unique vocals sound amazing, but accessible, in classic emo fashion, and are paired up with the perfect flavor of instrumentation to make a record that hits hard, and hits right where it hurts.

Syd Matters - Someday We Will Foresee Obstacles (2005)

Syd Matters’ sound sits somewhere between Radiohead’s orchestration and Elliott Smith’s earnest and honest vocals. Their 2005 album creates a vast atmosphere of space in whatever room you listen to it in. “To All of You,” “Flow Backwards,” and “English Way” are but a few of the certifiable bangers this album has to offer. I have recommendations to enhance your experience with it, but this is a school assignment.

The Unicorns - Who Will Cut Our Hair When We’re Gone? (2003)

Those who know ball will recognize this from its mention on How I Met Your Mother. I am not exaggerating when I say that this is one of the best indie rock albums I have heard. The diversity of sounds, the tasteful experimentation, and the tragic scarcity of it being The Unicorns’ only LP together make this album an experience like no other. Do yourself a favor.

Winnetka Bowling League - Sha La La (2024)

For the 1975 fans disappointed with their exclusion. Self described as ‘sorry-we’re-here-core,’ Winnetka Bowling League is one of the cleanest, tightest indie rock bands of today, with a sound that evokes The 1975, but with an identity that is distinctly their own. Tracks like “No One’s Ever Kissed You,” “America In Your 20’s,” and of course the title track, make this album a certified banger. And there’s no skips, either!

Fionn - scum. (2025)

An absolutely beautiful release from a pair of indie folk pop twins from Vancouver. What more could you want? That’s a rhetorical question, and the answer is MORE, because this album has zero skips. Once you’ve listened to it, you might consider giving their cover of System’s “Toxicity” a listen, which is also just hauntingly beautiful. Some of the best musicians today.

Dinosaur Jr. - You’re Living All Over Me (1987)

Dinosaur Jr. were pioneers for today’s indie rock sound. Their album You’re Living All Over Me employs a raw, heavy grunge energy that feels fresh and different even today. If you’re a fan of Nirvana, Alice In Chains, Queens of the Stone Age, and the like, you should definitely give this one a spin.

Plumtree - Mass Teen Fainting (1995)

Plumtree is an all girl indie rock band from Halifax that tragically disbanded in the year 2000. They are best known for their song “Scott Pilgrim,” which inspired Bryan Lee O’Malley to start the popular graphic novel series in 2004. Mass Teen Fainting was their debut album, from before “Scott Pilgrim,” and it features the same signature quirky, energetic vibe, but on clean guitars and with more focus on rhythm.

highspeedcrash - CRASHTEST (2025)

A Burlington, Ontario born quintet of the hyperpop persuasion, highspeedcrash creates beats and bangers of the highest quality in spite of their modest indie limitations. Bangers like “SLIDE” and “ANTHEM” off of their debut album cause one to wonder why they haven’t gotten bigger than they have, so hop on quick while they’re still niche!
